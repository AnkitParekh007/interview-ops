# Senior Frontend Engineer Track

## Who This Is For
Senior frontend engineers with 5–10 years of experience who are targeting Staff, Senior, or Lead Frontend roles at product companies, scale-ups, or enterprise engineering teams.

## Expected Skills
- Deep expertise in at least one major framework (Angular or React)
- TypeScript proficiency (generics, utility types, strict mode)
- Performance optimization (bundle size, rendering, network)
- Accessibility (WCAG 2.1, ARIA, screen reader testing)
- Component architecture and design system thinking
- State management (NgRx, Zustand, Redux, signals, context)
- Testing (unit, integration, e2e — Jest, Playwright, Cypress)
- API integration (REST, GraphQL, WebSockets, streaming)
- Mentoring and cross-functional collaboration
- CI/CD and deployment awareness

## Interview Focus Areas
1. Component architecture — reusability, composition, separation of concerns
2. State management — when to use what, tradeoffs, scalability
3. Performance — Core Web Vitals, lazy loading, memoization, profiling
4. Accessibility — real-world implementation, not just theory
5. Design systems — token systems, theming, component libraries
6. API integration — error handling, loading states, retries, caching
7. Testing strategy — test pyramid, what to test, how to structure tests
8. Mentoring — how you uplift junior engineers
9. Technical decisions — how you influence architecture
10. Tradeoffs — being able to articulate why you chose X over Y

## Common Interview Rounds
- **Recruiter Screen** (30 min): Motivation, level fit, comp alignment, role overview
- **Behavioral Round** (45–60 min): Leadership, conflict, ownership, failure/learning
- **Frontend Architecture** (60 min): Design a component system or complex UI feature
- **Coding Round** (60–90 min): Algorithmic or frontend-specific problem
- **System Design** (60 min): High-level system with frontend considerations
- **Project Deep Dive** (60 min): Walk through your most complex project
- **Team/Culture Fit** (30 min): Manager or team round

## Common Weak Spots
- Accessibility treated as afterthought rather than first-class concern
- Unable to articulate tradeoffs clearly (just says "it depends" without reasoning)
- Performance knowledge is theoretical, not hands-on
- Can't describe mentoring impact concretely
- Design system experience is surface-level
- Testing philosophy inconsistent with actual practice
- Underemphasizes collaboration and cross-functional influence

## Seniority Expectations
At senior level, interviewers expect:
- You identify edge cases without being prompted
- You ask clarifying questions before diving in
- You think about maintainability, not just making it work
- You speak to team impact, not just individual work
- You demonstrate strong opinions, loosely held
- You proactively think about failure modes
- You can explain complex concepts simply

## Example Question Categories
- "Walk me through how you'd architect a real-time data dashboard in Angular/React."
- "How do you approach performance optimization for a slow-loading SPA?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to push back on a product decision."
- "How do you make accessibility a team habit, not an individual effort?"
- "What's the most complex state management problem you've solved?"
- "How do you structure a front-end for testability from day one?"
- "Walk me through a project where you made a significant architectural decision."

## Recommended Modes
- behavioral
- frontend-architecture
- project-deep-dive
- coding
- system-design

## Evaluation Criteria
- Technical depth (not just breadth)
- Communication clarity under pressure
- System thinking at the component and application level
- Demonstrated leadership and mentoring
- Practical accessibility and performance knowledge
- Test strategy and code quality discipline
