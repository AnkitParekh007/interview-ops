# Developer Relations Engineer Track

## Who This Is For
DevRel Engineers and Developer Advocates targeting roles at developer tool companies, API platforms, cloud providers, and open-source organizations. This track balances technical depth with communication excellence.

## Expected Skills
- Technical credibility — can write and explain production-quality code
- Content creation — blog posts, tutorials, YouTube videos, livestreams
- Public speaking — conference talks, webinars, meetups, demos
- Community building — Discord/Slack management, forums, GitHub presence
- Documentation — writing clear, accurate API docs, getting started guides
- SDK and API experience — integrating with APIs, building demos
- Feedback loops — translating developer pain into product improvements
- OSS contribution — pull requests, issues, community PRs review
- Social media — Twitter/X, LinkedIn, technical content that gets engagement
- Product understanding — deeply using the product you advocate for
- Empathy — understanding developer frustration, meeting devs where they are

## Interview Focus Areas
1. Technical demo skills — can you build and explain a compelling live demo?
2. Content portfolio — quality and reach of existing content
3. Community impact — how have you built or grown a developer community?
4. Feedback translation — how do you bring developer feedback to the product team?
5. Public speaking — conference experience, comfort on stage or camera
6. Technical depth — genuinely deep in at least one relevant technical area
7. Writing quality — can you write clearly for a technical audience?
8. API/SDK experience — building with developer tools, finding friction
9. Collaboration — working with engineering, product, marketing, sales
10. Metrics — how do you measure DevRel impact? What does success look like?

## Common Interview Rounds
- **Recruiter Screen** (30 min): Portfolio review, motivation, team fit
- **Content Review** (45 min): Walk through your best posts, talks, demos
- **Live Demo** (45 min): Build a small demo with the company's product
- **Technical Screen** (45 min): Depth check in your primary technical area
- **Cross-Functional** (45 min): How you work with product and engineering
- **Community Panel** (30 min): Questions from community or team members

## Common Weak Spots
- Strong on social media but weak technical depth
- Great demos but no community building experience
- No metrics or data to support DevRel impact claims
- Struggle to articulate feedback loops to product team
- Content is tutorials, no thought leadership or opinion pieces
- Demo environment is fragile — breaks live
- No experience contributing to OSS or reviewing community PRs

## Seniority Expectations
- Senior/Staff: Own developer strategy, lead community initiatives, influence product roadmap
- Mid: Independently produce content and demos, manage community channels
- Junior: Support with content, events assistance, community moderation

## Example Question Categories
- "What's the most successful piece of content you've published? Why did it work?"
- "Walk me through a demo you'd give a developer audience for our product."
- "How do you turn developer frustration into a product improvement?"
- "Tell me about a community you built or significantly grew."
- "How do you measure the ROI of your DevRel work?"
- "What's your feedback loop from community to product?"
- "Tell me about a talk you gave. What went well? What would you change?"

## Recommended Modes
- behavioral
- project-deep-dive
- recruiter-screen
- candidate-questions
- coding

## Evaluation Criteria
- Technical credibility and depth
- Content creation quality and reach
- Community building and empathy
- Feedback loop effectiveness
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Communication excellence (written and verbal)
