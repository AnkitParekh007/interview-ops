# Engineering Manager Track

## Who This Is For
Engineering Managers and aspiring managers targeting EM or technical lead roles. Covers people management, technical strategy, delivery leadership, and cross-functional collaboration.

## Expected Skills
- People management — 1:1s, performance reviews, growth plans, difficult conversations
- Hiring — building pipelines, structured interviewing, leveling, offers
- Delivery — planning, estimation, unblocking teams, managing dependencies
- Technical strategy — architecture input, technical roadmaps, managing tech debt
- Cross-functional collaboration — product, design, data, infra, leadership
- Incident management — on-call culture, postmortems, blameless culture
- Team health — psychological safety, retention, engagement, burnout prevention
- Metrics — DORA metrics, velocity, quality, team satisfaction
- Career development — growth frameworks, leveling, promotion advocacy
- Organizational design — team topology, reporting structures, scaling teams
- Engineering culture — code review practices, testing culture, documentation standards

## Interview Focus Areas
1. People leadership — how you run 1:1s, handle underperformance, develop reports
2. Hiring and leveling — how you assess candidates, calibrate levels, close offers
3. Delivery management — planning, estimation, handling scope creep, dependencies
4. Technical judgment — architecture input without being in the code daily
5. Cross-functional — working with product and design, navigating disagreements
6. Incident response — how you handle outages, postmortems, on-call culture
7. Difficult conversations — underperformance, PIPs, conflict between engineers
8. Career development — how you grow engineers on your team
9. Metrics and impact — how you measure team health and delivery effectiveness
10. Culture building — what engineering culture looks like on your team

## Common Interview Rounds
- **Recruiter Screen** (30 min): Management experience, team size, motivation
- **Behavioral Round** (60 min): Leadership stories, difficult situations
- **Technical Depth** (45 min): Architecture discussions, technical judgment
- **Cross-Functional** (45 min): Product/design collaboration, stakeholder management
- **Team Scenarios** (60 min): Case studies, hypothetical management situations
- **Executive Round** (30–45 min): Strategy, vision, cultural alignment
- **Skip-Level** (30 min): Meeting potential direct reports

## Common Weak Spots
- Manager who avoids difficult conversations
- Strong IC background but unable to let go of being in the code
- No structured hiring process — "gut feeling" hiring
- No clear growth frameworks for their team
- Delivery focused but ignores team health and morale
- Can't articulate how they've influenced engineering culture
- No incident response experience or blameless retrospective culture
- Metrics are output-focused (story points) not outcome-focused

## Seniority Expectations
- Senior EM: Manage multiple teams or a complex team, influence organizational strategy
- EM: Own one team fully — hiring, delivery, growth, culture
- TL/M: Technical leadership plus some management responsibility

## Example Question Categories
- "Tell me about a time you had to let someone go. How did you handle it?"
- "How do you run your 1:1s? What's your format and cadence?"
- "How do you handle a high-performing engineer who is difficult to work with?"
- "Tell me about a project that went off the rails. How did you course-correct?"
- "How do you balance technical debt against feature delivery?"
- "Tell me about someone you've grown significantly in their career."
- "How do you decide whether to hire for a skill vs train for it?"
- "What does a healthy engineering culture look like on your team?"

## Recommended Modes
- behavioral
- project-deep-dive
- system-design
- candidate-questions
- recruiter-screen

## Evaluation Criteria
- People leadership quality and empathy
- Delivery track record and discipline
- Technical credibility and judgment
- Culture building and team health focus
- Hiring and leveling rigor
- Cross-functional collaboration effectiveness
