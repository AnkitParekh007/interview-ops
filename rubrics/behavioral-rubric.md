# Behavioral Interview Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **1 — Weak**: Answer was vague, missing key elements, or demonstrated significant gaps
- **2 — Below Expectations**: Partially answered but missing depth, specificity, or key qualities
- **3 — Meets Expectations**: Competent answer that covers the basics; what a qualified candidate provides
- **4 — Strong**: Clear, specific, impactful answer with concrete examples and measurable outcomes
- **5 — Exceptional**: Outstanding answer showing rare depth, self-awareness, and compelling story

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Communication Clarity (1–5)
Is the answer easy to follow? Is the structure logical? Can the interviewer track the story without effort?
- 1: Rambling, hard to follow, unclear narrative
- 3: Coherent story, minor tangents, generally understandable
- 5: Crisp, structured, engaging narrative that respects the interviewer's time

### 2. STAR Structure (1–5)
Does the answer include Situation, Task, Action, and Result?
- 1: Missing 2+ elements; mostly vague generalities
- 3: All elements present but Result is weak or missing metrics
- 5: All elements present with compelling context, clear personal action, and measurable result

### 3. Specificity and Examples (1–5)
Does the answer use real, specific examples or rely on vague claims?
- 1: "We always did X" / "I generally try to..." — no specific instance
- 3: One real example but details are thin
- 5: Vivid, specific example with dates, scale, decisions, and named technologies

### 4. Ownership and Accountability (1–5)
Does the candidate use "I" and take clear personal ownership?
- 1: Mostly "we" — can't identify individual contribution
- 3: Mix of "I" and "we"; personal contribution is present but not prominent
- 5: Clear "I" framing; owns successes AND mistakes without blaming others

### 5. Self-Awareness and Learning (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate honest reflection and growth?
- 1: No reflection; "it went great" or blame-shifting
- 3: Acknowledges one thing they'd change but doesn't go deep
- 5: Nuanced honest reflection with concrete lessons applied afterward

### 6. Leadership Signal (1–5)
Does the answer demonstrate leadership appropriate to their seniority level?
- 1: Passive role; no initiative or influence demonstrated
- 3: Led a specific task but not a broader initiative
- 5: Drove significant outcomes, influenced others, navigated ambiguity confidently

### 7. Collaboration (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate ability to work well with others — including through conflict?
- 1: Solo narrative; no mention of others or team dynamics
- 3: References team but collaboration is surface-level
- 5: Rich description of cross-functional dynamics, conflict resolution, and influence without authority

### 8. Seniority Signal (1–5)
Does the scope and impact of the story match the level being hired for?
- 1: Story is appropriate for a junior, applying for senior
- 3: Story matches the level with some gaps
- 5: Story demonstrates clear senior/staff-level scope, complexity, and organizational impact
