# Project Deep Dive Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **1 — Weak**: Can't tell a coherent technical story about their work
- **2 — Below Expectations**: Story present but lacking depth, ownership, or impact
- **3 — Meets Expectations**: Solid project walk-through with adequate depth
- **4 — Strong**: Compelling story with technical depth and demonstrated leadership
- **5 — Exceptional**: Outstanding narrative that makes the interviewer want to hire them

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Story Structure (1–5)
Is the project walk-through organized and easy to follow?
- 1: Chronological dump with no narrative arc; confusing
- 3: Clear context, challenge, and resolution; some gaps
- 5: Compelling narrative with clear problem, journey, decision points, and resolution

### 2. Technical Depth (1–5)
How deep is the technical knowledge demonstrated?
- 1: High-level only; can't go deep on any component
- 3: Good depth on primary components; some shallow areas
- 5: Deep on all major components; reveals implementation details that prove genuine authorship

### 3. Decision Rationale (1–5)
Can the candidate explain why they made the architectural and technical decisions they did?
- 1: "We just chose that" — no rationale given
- 3: Explains primary decisions; some choices unexplained
- 5: Clear reasoning for every major decision; explicitly names alternatives that were rejected and why

### 4. Metrics and Impact (1–5)
Does the candidate have real numbers for outcomes?
- 1: Purely qualitative outcomes ("it went well")
- 3: Some metrics present (p99 latency, user count, error rate reduction)
- 5: Compelling quantified impact (X% improvement, Y% reduction, $Z impact) with attribution

### 5. Team Dynamics (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate effective collaboration and leadership?
- 1: Solo narrative; team is invisible
- 3: Team mentioned; some description of collaboration or conflict
- 5: Rich team narrative — mentoring, conflict resolution, cross-functional coordination, unblocking others

### 6. Lessons Learned (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate genuine reflection and growth?
- 1: No lessons; "we nailed it" or defensive framing
- 3: One or two surface lessons mentioned
- 5: Honest, specific lessons applied to real subsequent decisions; demonstrates growth mindset

### 7. Seniority Signal (1–5)
Does the project scope and personal impact reflect the level being hired for?
- 1: Project is too small or impact is individual-level for a senior role
- 3: Project scope aligns with level; impact is modest
- 5: Project demonstrates clear senior/staff-level scope — multi-team, high-stakes, or foundational
