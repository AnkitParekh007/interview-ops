# Recruiter Screen Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **1 — Weak**: Clear misalignment or communication issues
- **2 — Below Expectations**: Some concerns; missing preparation or role clarity
- **3 — Meets Expectations**: Reasonable candidate who passes the bar for next round
- **4 — Strong**: Well-prepared, clear communicator with good role fit signals
- **5 — Exceptional**: Stands out immediately; leaves the recruiter eager to champion

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Role Fit Clarity (1–5)
Does the candidate's background align with the role requirements?
- 1: Clear mismatch — wrong level, wrong skills, wrong function
- 3: Adequate fit; some gaps but transferable skills present
- 5: Strong alignment — background maps directly to role requirements and level

### 2. Motivation Authenticity (1–5)
Is the candidate's interest in the role and company genuine and specific?
- 1: Generic interest; couldn't name anything specific about the role or company
- 3: General interest in the opportunity; some company awareness
- 5: Specific, researched interest — references the company's product, mission, or team

### 3. Communication Quality (1–5)
Is the candidate a clear, concise, professional communicator?
- 1: Rambling, hard to follow, uses excessive filler words
- 3: Communicates adequately; some tangents
- 5: Clear, direct, professional — would represent well in cross-functional meetings

### 4. Location and Availability (1–5)
Is the candidate's logistics compatible with the role?
- 1: Clear blocker — wrong timezone, wrong work authorization, unavailable timeline
- 3: Compatible with caveats — some flexibility needed
- 5: Full alignment — start date, location, and work authorization are unambiguous

### 5. Culture Alignment (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate values and working style aligned with the team?
- 1: Red flags — entitlement, inflexibility, or culture mismatch signals
- 3: Neutral — no strong culture signals either way
- 5: Strong alignment signals — collaboration, ownership, growth mindset evident

### 6. Enthusiasm (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate genuine excitement about the opportunity?
- 1: Flat, disengaged, or clearly testing the market passively
- 3: Interested but not noticeably enthusiastic
- 5: Genuinely excited — asks great questions, expresses specific interest
