# Senior Engineer Holistic Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **1 — Weak**: Significant gaps for a senior-level engineer
- **2 — Below Expectations**: Meets some senior-level bars but inconsistently
- **3 — Meets Expectations**: Solid senior-level performance
- **4 — Strong**: Above bar for senior; approaches staff-level
- **5 — Exceptional**: Exceptionally rare; top-of-range for the level

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Technical Depth (1–5)
Does the candidate demonstrate deep expertise in their stated domain?
- 1: Surface-level knowledge only; can't go deep when probed
- 3: Solid depth in primary area; adequate in adjacent areas
- 5: Exceptional depth; reveals nuances and edge cases most seniors don't know

### 2. System Thinking (1–5)
Does the candidate think beyond their immediate task to the broader system?
- 1: Tactical thinking only; no system-level awareness
- 3: Sees the larger system; mentions scalability and reliability
- 5: Designs for system-wide properties from the start; considers team, ops, and future maintainers

### 3. Mentoring and Influence (1–5)
Has the candidate demonstrated a track record of growing others and influencing technical direction?
- 1: No evidence of mentoring or cross-team influence
- 3: Has mentored juniors; some technical influence on immediate team
- 5: Measurable impact on multiple engineers' careers; influenced architectural direction beyond their team

### 4. Ownership (1–5)
Does the candidate take full ownership of their work and its outcomes?
- 1: Blames process, tools, or teammates for failures
- 3: Generally owns their work; occasionally diffuses responsibility
- 5: Radical ownership — takes responsibility for outcomes, proactively unblocks, drives to completion

### 5. Cross-Functional Collaboration (1–5)
Can the candidate work effectively with product, design, data, and leadership?
- 1: Engineers-only worldview; no evidence of product or design collaboration
- 3: Works cross-functionally but in a reactive mode
- 5: Proactively drives alignment; has good product instincts; influences non-technical stakeholders

### 6. Decision Making (1–5)
Does the candidate make high-quality decisions under uncertainty?
- 1: Analysis paralysis or impulsive decisions without reasoning
- 3: Makes reasonable decisions with adequate reasoning
- 5: Clear decision framework; weighs tradeoffs explicitly; right on the hard calls

### 7. Coding Quality (1–5)
Is the candidate's code readable, maintainable, and appropriate to the problem?
- 1: Clever but unreadable; or correct but unmaintainable
- 3: Clean, functional code; some room for improvement
- 5: Code that others want to read; sets the bar for the team

### 8. Communication (1–5)
Can the candidate communicate complex ideas clearly to different audiences?
- 1: Jargon-heavy or vague; loses non-technical audiences
- 3: Communicates well with peers; some gaps with non-technical stakeholders
- 5: Adapts communication to audience; can explain complex systems simply and compellingly
