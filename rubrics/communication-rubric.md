# Communication Rubric

## Scoring Scale
- **1 — Weak**: Communication impedes understanding; major barriers
- **2 — Below Expectations**: Communicates but with notable gaps
- **3 — Meets Expectations**: Effective communication for the role
- **4 — Strong**: Clear, structured, confident communicator
- **5 — Exceptional**: Outstanding communicator who makes complex ideas feel simple

---

## Dimensions

### 1. Clarity (1–5)
Is the communication easy to understand?
- 1: Confusing, contradictory, or impossible to follow
- 3: Understandable with effort; occasional unclear passages
- 5: Crystal clear; every sentence advances understanding

### 2. Structure (1–5)
Is the communication organized logically?
- 1: Rambling, non-linear, jumps between topics
- 3: Generally structured; some tangents
- 5: Perfectly organized — context, body, conclusion; easy to follow at speed

### 3. Conciseness (1–5)
Does the candidate use words efficiently?
- 1: Verbose to the point of obscuring the message
- 3: Reasonably concise with some over-explanation
- 5: Respects the interviewer's time; says more with less

### 4. Listening and Response (1–5)
Does the candidate listen to questions carefully and answer what was asked?
- 1: Answers a different question; talks over the interviewer
- 3: Answers the question with occasional tangents or misunderstandings
- 5: Active listening; pauses to think; directly addresses the exact question

### 5. Confidence (1–5)
Does the candidate communicate with appropriate confidence?
- 1: Undermines every statement with excessive hedging ("I think maybe...")
- 3: Generally confident; some unnecessary qualifiers
- 5: Confident without arrogance; says "I don't know" clearly when appropriate

### 6. Technical Vocabulary (1–5)
Does the candidate use appropriate technical language?
- 1: Vague, non-technical language for technical concepts
- 3: Correct terminology in most cases; occasional misuse
- 5: Precise, appropriate vocabulary; explains trade-off between jargon and clarity for the audience
